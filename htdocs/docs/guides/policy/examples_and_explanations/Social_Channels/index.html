Multiple Stories via a Single Share Option

Publishing multiple Stream stories via a single share option can be an effective tool in enabling communication among friends and creating a more natural sharing flow for the user. For example, it would be appropriate to allow a user to post a unique message to a friend’s wall in addition to the user’s own wall without requiring the user to click on two share options. Instead, users can confirm with a single share option their intent to post more than one story at a time, provided they know who they are sharing with and the content of the story is consistent with their expectations. Examples of positive use cases are enabling users to share gift cards, events, and concert tickets that they have purchased for more than one friend, without requiring the user to click on multiple share options.

Please note the importance of using discretion when providing users with the option to publish more than one story at a time, as this functionality may result in negative user feedback which could exceed our spam thresholds. Repetitive stories often receive negative user feedback, particularly if posted in high volumes over a short period of time. We highly encourage limited use of this functionality and continued review of the Insights page to ensure that users are responding positively to all Stream stories generated by your app. Additionally, it remains a best practice to utilize the Request Channel as a means for users to invite friends to your app.

Driving Reengagement – Action Links

When designing a Stream story, it’s important to note that a custom Action Link can be added next to Like and Comment links to drive users towards a specific destination. This is an effective means to elicit a call to action from both new and existing users.

Open Graph – Action Links

Open Graph Action Links are customizable links that provide a way for people to take an action in your app directly from within News Feed, Timeline, or Ticker. As a best practice, Action Links should replicate an authentic action users can take in your app.

Please do not use any wording that doesn’t properly represent an experience from your app, and do not include advertisements or cross-promotion (such as “powered by [advertiser]” or “sponsored by [advertiser]”). Action Links do not need to point users to any specific location, but if they do direct the user to a location, it needs to be to the same app that generated the story.

In addition, Action Links should not emulate actions that could be easily mistakable as a Facebook action. Please do not use the following terms: Like, Unlike, Comment, Share, Sponsored, Follow Post, Unfollow Post, Read, Watch or Listen.
