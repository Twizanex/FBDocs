Publishing Past Actions

Core Concepts › Open Graph › Publishing Past Actions

With Open Graph, users of your app can choose to have both their present and past activities published to their Timelines. For actions that occurred in the past, people can add past information from your app to the correct point on their timelines.

Open Graph gracefully allows for these cases without having to change the way you publish current actions; it's simply controlled by setting the start_time on your action to the appropriate date in the past. For example:

POST /me/{app-namespace}:eat?
       dessert=http://www.example.com/gelato.html&
       start_time=2011-11-13T16:21:35+0000&
       access_token=YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN

When you publish any action and set the start_time to be more than 3 days earlier from the current time, it will act a little differently than actions that are taken in the present. Specifically, past actions will not be displayed on the user's Ticker or News Feed. Rather, they will be shown in the user's Timeline at the appropriate past date. Aggregations will continue to populate as defined by the developer.

To add past actions, your application must prompt a user with explicit controls before posting them back to the user's Timeline. Transparency and control will help inform a user's expectations of past actions that would appear in the user's Timeline. In the example below, a book recommendations website has created a separate page with "Add to Timeline" buttons for users to publish past review actions back to their Timelines.

Notifications

Posting past actions will generate a notification for the user indicating that a past action has been posted to their Timeline. This helps the user understand that they might not see the action in their current Timeline view, but that they should scroll through their Timeline or look at their Activity Log for more details.

Our platform will only show a single notification for a given action-app pair. For example, if a user performed actions in an app that allowed that app to post 4 past action's to the user's Timeline, the user would only see a single notification that would say "<App Name> added 4 books you read in the past to your timeline". The notification would link directly to the app tab for that app, which would show only the Timeline aggregations and activity for that app.

App Tab

From this app tab view, a user has the ability to quickly remove any actions that were posted. Removing a posted action is one factor that indicates relevancy for your app's overall distribution, so it is in your best interest to properly and clearly inform a user when your app posts any past, present, or future actions. Giving the user more granular control on what is posted from your app is often the best option.

Activity Log

As with any action posted to a user's Timeline, all past actions will show up in a user's Activity Log. The one difference is that the stories posted in the Activity Log for past actions will have a special icon (a small clock) associated with the story. This symbol helps users immediately identify past actions that may not show up in their current Timeline views. If users mouseover the clock icon, they will see the exact date for which that past action was posted.
